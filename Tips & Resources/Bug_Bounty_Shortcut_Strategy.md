The source provides a detailed strategy, or "shortcut," for maximizing income in bug bounty hunting by focusing on the business side and leveraging specific timing, arguing that most beginners waste significant time for minimal returns.

### The Problem with the Standard Approach

Many beginner bug bounty hunters make the mistake of picking a target, running extensive reconnaissance tools (like Burp Suite, Subfinder, and Aamass), and spending weeks testing endpoints. This typically results in either finding absolutely nothing or uncovering a low-severity vulnerability, such as an XSS, that might pay $200. If a researcher invests 60 hours for a $200 payout, they are essentially working for a low minimum wage ($3 an hour). This low return happens because they are doing what everyone else is doing, meaning those easy vulnerabilities were likely found six months prior.

### The Golden Window Shortcut

The recommended shortcut involves stopping the waste of time on general reconnaissance and focusing exclusively on **newly launched programs and newly acquired assets**. When a company launches a new bug bounty program or acquires another company, there is a "golden window" of **2 to 4 weeks** where the target is heavily loaded with vulnerabilities because nobody has had sufficient time to test the assets.

During this critical window:
*   Competition is lower.
*   Vulnerabilities are widespread.
*   The company is motivated to pay out quickly to build a good reputation with security researchers.

### Exploiting the Opportunity

To successfully exploit this opportunity, hunters must set up monitoring for three specific areas:

1.  **New Programs:** Monitor programs launching on platforms like BugCrowd, HackerOne, and Integrity, which can be automated using their APIs.
2.  **Company Acquisitions:** Monitor tech news sites for announcements of company acquisitions, as the newly acquired assets are always added to bug bounty scopes.
3.  **New Subdomains:** Look for new subdomains appearing on existing programs using tools such as Certificate Transparency Logs or Sublert.

When an opportunity appears, a researcher needs to move **fast—within hours, not days**—as other hunters who know this shortcut will be doing the same.

### Turning Bug Bounties into Sustainable Income

The true financial benefit comes from understanding the business side, which can be worth "literally tens of thousands of dollars". Instead of walking away after submitting a valid bug report on a newly launched program, hunters should capitalize on the company's "damage control mode".

The strategy involves two key steps:

1.  **Consulting Engagement:** Immediately follow up on the confirmed vulnerability report with a professional email offering to conduct a comprehensive security assessment for a fixed fee. This should be positioned as helping the company prevent other researchers from inevitably finding more issues, emphasizing that the hunter already understands their infrastructure from finding the first bug. This step can turn a $500 bug bounty payment into a $5,000 consulting contract.
2.  **Building Relationships:** After completing the initial consulting work, top hunters stay in contact with the security team, offering to be their "go-to researcher" for future acquisitions or product launches. This relationship building leads to the security team reaching out to them *before* adding new assets to the public program, granting the hunter **exclusive first access** and paying them directly.

### The True Key to Success

The source emphasizes that high-earning bug bounty hunters are not constantly grinding public programs and competing with thousands of researchers. They have established relationships that pay them directly for priority access. The technical skills required for finding vulnerabilities are merely the **entry fee**. The real money stems from knowing how to scale a workflow, how to write reports for maximum payouts, knowing which programs pay fast, and, most importantly, **building relationships with security teams** (the business model that others ignore).

***

This business-focused approach is likened to having a **VIP pass** to a major event. While everyone else waits in the general line (grinding old programs) and gets minimal access, those with the shortcut leverage timing and relationships (the VIP pass) to gain immediate, exclusive access to the best opportunities, ensuring higher payouts and sustainable revenue.
